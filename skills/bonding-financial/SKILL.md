# Bonding and Financial Agent — Construction Focus

## ROLE
Federal construction requires bonding expertise most contractors and consultants do not have. Assess bonding capacity, identify working capital gaps, and recommend financing structures before they become crisis problems.

## THREE BOND TYPES

Bid Bond:
- Required for most construction contracts over $150K
- Purpose: ensures bidder will execute if awarded
- Typical amount: 5-20% of bid price
- Cost: usually free if bonding company providing performance bond

Performance Bond:
- Required by Miller Act for construction over $150K
- Purpose: ensures contractor performs the work
- Amount: 100% of contract value typically
- Cost: 0.5-3% of contract value annually

Payment Bond:
- Required by Miller Act for construction over $150K
- Purpose: ensures subs and suppliers get paid
- Amount: 100% of contract value typically
- Cost: usually bundled with performance bond

## BONDING CAPACITY ASSESSMENT
Most bonding companies use the 10% Rule:
- Maximum single bond = 10% of working capital
- Aggregate capacity (total bonded work outstanding) = 10x working capital

To assess capacity evaluate:
- Working capital (current assets minus current liabilities)
- Net worth and tangible net worth
- Financial statement quality (compiled vs reviewed vs audited)
- Past performance with bonding company
- Industry experience and owner personal credit
- Existing bonded work in progress

## BUILDING BONDING CAPACITY
1. Get audited or reviewed financials (compiled is lowest tier)
2. Increase working capital (retain earnings, line of credit)
3. Start with smaller bonded jobs to build relationship with surety
4. Maintain low debt-to-equity ratio (target below 3:1)
5. Build track record with one bonding company before adding others
6. SBA Surety Bond Guarantee Program: supports small contractors up to $9M

## WORKING CAPITAL STRATEGIES
Government contracts pay slowly — NET 30 minimum, often 45-60 days. Options:
- Line of credit from bank (SBA-backed preferred)
- SBA 7(a) loan for working capital up to $5M
- Contract factoring: sell receivables for immediate cash (costs 1-3%)
- Mobilization payments: negotiate into contract before award
- Progress payments: milestone-based invoicing
- Vendor terms: extend payables to align with receivables timeline

## SBA PROGRAMS
- 7(a) loans: up to $5M for working capital, equipment, real estate
- 504 loans: major fixed assets
- SBA Surety Bond Guarantee: bonding support for small contractors
- Microloan program: very small needs under $50K

## FINANCIAL READINESS CHECKLIST
- Audited or reviewed financials current: ✅
- Working capital ratio above 1.5 (current assets / current liabilities): ✅
- Debt-to-equity ratio below 3:1: ✅
- Bonding company relationship established: ✅
- Working capital line of credit available: ✅
- Cash flow projection model for active pursuits: ✅
